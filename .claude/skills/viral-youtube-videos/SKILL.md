# Viral YouTube Videos — the Learn By Leo method

A complete, applied playbook for growing a YouTube channel, distilled verbatim
from all of Leo Schluper's (Learn By Leo) teaching videos. Use it to coach the
user, generate assets (ideas, titles, thumbnails, hooks, scripts), and
critique drafts against the exact tactics the best creators use.

## The one idea everything hangs on

> The algorithm only reflects what people want. If people **click** and
> **watch**, YouTube promotes the video more. That is *all* the algorithm does.

So there are exactly two jobs, and every technique serves one of them:

1. **Get the click (CTR)** — the idea, the title, the thumbnail.
2. **Keep the watch (retention)** — the hook, the story, the script, the edit.

Doing either is easy. Doing both **better than every other video next to yours**
is the whole game. A video is never judged alone — there are always ~10 other
interesting videos one click away, at every second.

order: **idea → title & thumbnail → hook → script/retention → edit**, always
making the **title and thumbnail *before* writing the script** (see below).

## Non-negotiable rules (apply these everywhere)

- **Make the title and thumbnail BEFORE you write or shoot anything.** They are
  how you *calculate whether the idea is even good*. If you can't create a clear,
  compelling reason to click, the idea is dead — pick another. Everything
  downstream (hook, script, edit) exists to deliver on that promise.
- **The idea must be broad.** It has to appeal to *most* people in the niche, not
  a narrow sub-topic. A broad idea can out-earn a thousand narrow videos.
- **Every part of the video needs a reason to watch it**, stated *before* the
  payoff. Value the viewer doesn't know is coming is just boring information.
- **Clarity is the floor for everything.** Confusion ("I don't know what's
  happening") destroys every other tactic. Distinguish:
  - **Clarity** = "I know what's happening." → *required at all times.*
  - **Uncertainty** = "I don't know what happens next." → *this is the good stuff; it creates intrigue.*
  - **Confusion** = "I don't know what's happening." → *fatal; wash out immediately.*
- **Study your niche's outliers.** Find the top-performing videos your audience
  already watches, learn *what they value and why*, then deliver that value
  better than anyone else.
- **There is no such thing as an outro.** Think in terms of getting people to
  *binge* the channel — keep the second half as strong as the first, and never
  end by begging for likes/subs/money. That's not what the viewer came for.

## Coaching style (how Leo teaches — mirror it)

When you critique or advise, sound like the method itself:

- **Diagnose from the viewer's zero-context position, not the creator's.** The
  creator is biased — they have all the context. The viewer has none. Most
  "quality" problems are really *communication/confusion* problems.
- **Always give the concrete example or analogy**, not just the abstract rule.
  ("Variety is like seasoning: it brings out flavor, but too much ruins the
  dish.") Turn every abstract note into something vivid.
- **Point to what to cut.** If a section can't be given a reason to watch (can't
  create curiosity or an expectation), cut it or compress it to a sentence of
  voiceover.
- **Be specific and actionable.** Rewrite the line, name the tactic number,
  propose the exact thumbnail element — don't hand back vague encouragement.
